One of the highlights of my college career was my study abroad experience in Meknes, Morocco. The summer after my first year of college, I spent eight weeks living with a host family and taking four hours of Arabic language courses a day. My Arabic skills were put to the test on a daily basis, from communicating basic needs with my host family to navigating around the country. This summer course allowed me to advance a year in my language studies.

My batallion sent a group of us to White Sands Missile Range in New Mexico, and we rucked a marathon carrying 35 pounds. It was so encouraging to be surrounded by so many like-minded people.
